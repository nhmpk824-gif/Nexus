# Code of Conduct

Nexus follows the [Contributor Covenant, version 2.1](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct/).

In short: **be kind, assume good faith, and keep discussions focused on the work.** Disagreement on technical direction is welcome and expected — disagreement in a way that makes someone feel unwelcome is not.

This applies to every space the project touches: issues, pull requests, discussions, commit messages, and any other channel where people interact as part of Nexus.

## Reporting

If something happens that you think crosses the line, let the maintainer know. There are two ways:

- **Private** (preferred for anything sensitive): open a [private security advisory](https://github.com/FanyinLiu/Nexus/security/advisories/new). The advisory system works for conduct reports, not just vulnerabilities, and only the maintainer can see it.
- **Non-sensitive**: open a regular issue or tag the maintainer in a discussion.

Reports are read and acted on. If the situation warrants it, the maintainer may hide comments, close threads, or restrict repository access — proportional to what actually happened, and explained where possible.

## Scope

This applies across all Nexus project spaces, and also when someone is representing the project in public (for example, using an official account or speaking on the project's behalf at an event).
